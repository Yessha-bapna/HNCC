Academic Structure & Curriculum

A balanced curriculum designed to integrate management theory, practical application, and professional skill development.

Program Structure

The MBA curriculum at HNCC MBA follows a structured and progressive approach, enabling students to build strong foundations in management before advancing into specialized and applied learning.

The program is divided into core courses, electives, skill-based modules, and project work, ensuring comprehensive academic and professional development.

Curriculum Snapshot

  • Two Academic Years (Four Semesters)
  • Choice-Based Credit System
  • Core + Elective Structure
  • Industry-Oriented Curriculum
  • Project & Practical Exposure

Curriculum Framework

Core management disciplines combined with domain-specific learning.

Core Management Courses

Subjects such as Management Principles, Economics, Accounting, Marketing, Finance, Operations, and Human Resource Management.

Specialization Electives

Advanced courses aligned with chosen specialization areas, allowing focused and career-oriented learning.

Skill Development Modules

Communication skills, leadership development, analytical thinking, and professional readiness programs.

Teaching Methodology

Learning approaches that connect classroom concepts with real-world practice.

Case-Based Learning

Real business cases used to develop analytical and decision-making skills.

Industry Interaction

Guest lectures, seminars, and industry engagement for practical exposure.

Experiential Learning

Projects, presentations, and teamwork-based learning activities.

Assessment & Evaluation

Student performance is evaluated through a combination of internal assessments, presentations, assignments, examinations, and project work. The assessment system ensures continuous evaluation and academic rigor.
